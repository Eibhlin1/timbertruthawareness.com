FAQs

Answers to common questions about timber truth and more to help you get started smoothly.

What is timber truth?

Our website aims to raise awareness about deforestation. Urgent action is needed to halt this destruction; continuing is akin to harming our planet and jeopardizing our future.

Purpose of the website?

Deforestation, primarily caused by human activities like agriculture and logging, devastates ecosystems, accelerates climate change, and imperils biodiversity.

Why it is important?

Halting deforestation is vital for climate stability, biodiversity, water cycle regulation, soil protection, and sustainable resource management, ensuring a healthier planet for all.

How can we stop?

Together, we combat deforestation through sustainable land use, conservation, law enforcement, and responsible consumption of forest products.

Recent Issues

Last update on 4/27/2024

Impact to Climate Change

Deforestation exacerbates climate change by releasing stored carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, disrupting the carbon cycle. Loss of trees reduces carbon absorption, leading to increased greenhouse gas levels, global warming, altered weather patterns, and environmental instability.

Impacts to Biodiversity

Deforestation threatens biodiversity by destroying habitats, leading to species extinction and disrupting ecological balance, impacting both plant and animal diversity.

Impacts to Water Cycle

Deforestation disrupts the water cycle by reducing transpiration and evaporation, leading to altered rainfall patterns, soil erosion, decreased water quality, and increased risk of floods and droughts.

Soil Degraadation

Deforestation contributes to soil degradation by increasing erosion, reducing nutrient availability, and destabilizing soil structure, leading to decreased fertility, loss of arable land, and increased vulnerability to landslides.

Impacts to Animals

A study in a Ugandan forest revealed animals resorting to eating bat guano due to deforestation caused by tobacco farming. The guano contained various viruses, including one related to SARS-CoV-2, highlighting the risk of zoonotic disease transmission. Researchers urge intervention to prevent future pandemics by addressing wildlife habitat loss and human-wildlife interactions.
